The Details

The ceremony begins at 4:30 p.m. at Central Park in the Glebe, led by our Member of Parliament Paul Dewar.

A fantastic parade follows the ceremony. This is where all of our guests move as a group from the park to the reception hall, an easy five-minute walk.

The reception begins around 6 p.m., as soon as our parade lands us at the Glebe Community Centre. Ben and Elyse from Ottawa Streat Gourmet will put heaps of fresh, local food on the table for everyone. Yes - there will be tacos! David Moscrop will lead us through a series of hilarious and touching toasts. Local beer, sangria, and a selection of wines will flow all night at the open bar. And there will be lots and lots of dancing.

Dress Code

Most folks will dress semi-formal, but please wear whatever makes you comfortable and happy. We're going for a summer-dressy /garden party vibe.

The ceremony will be outside in the heat of a July afternoon, so wear something cool, and bring sunscreen. We suggest you skip high heels, since they will get stuck in the grass.

The reception hall is air-conditioned, so a wrap or sweater for later isn't a bad idea.

In case of rain

If it's really raining, we'll move the ceremony to the Glebe Community Centre. We'll make a call about the weather in the morning and advise everyone by email, so if the forecast looks wet, keep an eye on your inbox.

Kids are welcome

If you're bringing your kids, please let us know on your RSVP so we can adjust seating and find some cool toys for them to enjoy.

Dates are sweet - but...

This is a small event for our respective families and close friends, which means that there's reserved space only for those named on the invitation. We guarantee you'll know someone attending and we'll do our best to make the evening fun and inclusive for everyone.

While you can't bring a date, feel free to leave with one!
